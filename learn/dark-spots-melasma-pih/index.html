Guide: Dark spots, melasma & PIH

Uneven tone shows up differently depending on the trigger. This guide breaks down terminology and how a facial oil blend can complement your dermatologist’s plan.

Sevioraè blends focus on cosmetic appearance and comfort. They sit alongside SPF, targeted actives and lifestyle habits—never in place of professional care.

Understanding the terms

Dark spots

Umbrella phrase for localized patches of excess pigment that may follow sun exposure, blemishes or minor irritation.

Melasma

A chronic pigment condition often influenced by hormones and UV exposure. It typically appears in symmetric patterns and demands strict sun care plus medical oversight.

Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH)

Flat marks that remain once the original flare has healed. They can darken if skin is inflamed again, so barrier support and gentle routines matter.

Supportive cosmetic habits

  • Apply sunscreen every morning and reapply whenever you’re exposed to daylight.
  • Layer hydration (essence, serum, gel creams) before sealing with a few drops of facial oil.
  • Introduce dermatologist-recommended brightening actives slowly so skin stays calm.

Where the Sevioraè blend fits

The Dark Spot & Pigmentation Blend is being crafted with antioxidant-rich plant oils (think rosehip, sea buckthorn) and soothing essential oils. The texture is intentionally cushiony for evening massage without tugging skin.
