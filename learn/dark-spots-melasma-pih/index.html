Decoded: Dark Spots, Melasma & PIH

Uneven tone shows up differently depending on the trigger. This guide breaks down the terminology and how a facial oil blend complements professional care.

Reading time: 4 mins • Guide No. 02
Feature Image: Ingredients

Uneven skin tone is rarely just one thing. It is a spectrum. Sevioraè blends are formulated to focus on cosmetic appearance and comfort, sitting alongside SPF, targeted actives, and lifestyle habits—never in place of professional dermatological care.

The Glossary

Identifying the type of pigmentation is the first step in managing expectations.

☀

Dark Spots

The umbrella phrase for localized patches of excess pigment.

Trigger: Cumulative sun exposure or age.
〰

Melasma

A chronic condition appearing in symmetric, blotchy patterns.

Trigger: Hormones, heat, and UV light.
⚡

PIH

Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation. Flat marks left after a flare-up.

Trigger: Acne, bug bites, or trauma.

Supportive Cosmetic Habits

Pigmentation pathways are complex. A multi-modal approach is usually required to see visible changes.

  • The Non-Negotiable: SPF. UV rays darken existing pigment instantly. Apply sunscreen every morning and reapply whenever exposed to daylight.
  • The Sandwich Method. Layer hydration (essence, serums) first. Seal with your Sevioraè blend last. This traps moisture, which is vital for cellular turnover.
  • Patience with Actives. Introduce tyrosinase inhibitors or acids slowly. Inflammation causes pigment, so aggressive scrubbing often backfires.

The Role of Oil

The Dark Spot & Pigmentation Blend is crafted with antioxidant-rich plant oils (like Rosehip and Sea Buckthorn) known for their high Vitamin C and E content. The texture is intentionally cushiony, allowing for evening massage to stimulate blood flow without tugging sensitive skin.
