Guide: Acne vs. oily skin

Oily skin and acne-prone skin often overlap, yet they call for different textures, support ingredients and daily rituals.

This guide is a plain-language summary to help you choose between the Acne-Prone Skin Blend and the Oily Skin & Pore Balance Blend.

How they differ

Oily skin

Oily skin refers to consistent sebum production that leaves a sheen, visible pores or makeup slide. It does not automatically mean active breakouts are present. Support strategies focus on lightweight textures, micro-exfoliation and breathable layers.

Acne-prone skin

Acne-prone skin can be oily, combination or even dehydrated. It is defined by recurring breakouts, congestion and an increased risk of post-inflammatory marks. Here we look for calming botanicals, gentle clarity-support essential oils and rituals that do not overstimulate skin.

Mapping your routine

  • Keep the base routine gentle: non-stripping cleanse, water-based hydration, sunscreen daily.
  • Introduce actives (BHAs, retinoids) under the guidance of a professional; oils should support, not replace, these steps.
  • Use oils as the final or second-to-last step so they seal lightweight hydration without blocking pores.

Selecting a Sevioraè blend

If you’re mostly addressing shine and texture, start with the Oily Skin & Pore Balance Blend. For wider breakout support, reach for the Acne-Prone Skin Blend that layers clarifying essential oils with soothing carriers.

In both cases, patch test before applying to the face, and read the Science page to understand how we research essential and plant oils for these concerns.
