Guide: Skin barrier repair

Your barrier is the outermost layer that keeps water in and aggressors out. When it is compromised, everything feels reactive.

This guide summarizes the simple habits we lean on while formulating the Skin Barrier Repair Blend.

Signs your barrier wants a reset

  • Persistent tightness even after moisturizing.
  • Rough texture, flaking or a “shiny yet dry” look.
  • Increased stinging from products that previously felt fine.

Steps to create breathing room

Cut back on exfoliants and high-strength actives temporarily. Focus on gentle cleansing, hydrating mists or essences, ceramide-rich moisturizers and a protective layer of facial oil to seal it all in.

When reintroducing active ingredients, stagger them (e.g., acids one night, retinoids another) and monitor how skin feels the next morning.

How our blend is being shaped

We rely on barrier-friendly carriers such as oat oil, evening primrose and squalane, layering them with calming essential oils at low dilutions. The texture is glidey enough for mindful massage but still breathable for daytime use.

You’ll find more detail about these choices on the Science page, especially in the section on barrier support.
