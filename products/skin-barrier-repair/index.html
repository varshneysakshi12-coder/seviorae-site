Skin Barrier Repair Blend

Plush lipids, oat-derived soothers and a hint of blue tansy calm skin that feels tight, over-exfoliated or wind-chapped. Created for slow breathing exercises as much as topical comfort.

Launching alongside a usage guide on how to pause actives and reintroduce them mindfully.

What to expect

A comforting slip, subtle herbal-floral aroma and a protective film that seals hydration without feeling waxy. Expect a blue-green tint from azulene-rich oils.

Still a cosmetic product—work with your dermatologist for chronic inflammatory skin conditions.

What this blend is designed to support

  • Comforts skin that feels tight, flaky or reactive.
  • Helps maintain a flexible barrier film while active treatments are paused.
  • Encourages slow massage to reduce touch-related irritation.

Key components & rationale

  • Carrier oils: oat, evening primrose, squalane and moringa for lipid replenishment.
  • Essential oils: blue tansy and Roman chamomile at tiny dilutions for their calming aroma.
  • Supporting actives: ceramide-like complexes and vitamin E to reinforce the moisture barrier.

How this is expected to fit into a routine

Morning

Apply 1–2 drops over damp skin after a hydrating serum and before sunscreen when weather feels harsh.

Evening

Use 3–4 drops as the final step after moisturizer, gently pressing rather than rubbing.

Layering with actives

When reintroducing exfoliants or retinoids, apply the blend first as a buffer or mix a drop into your moisturizer for a diluted effect.

Safety notes

  • Patch test on compromised barriers is still essential—wait 24 hours before wider use.
  • Do not apply to open fissures or actively infected areas.
  • Work with a dermatologist if you suspect eczema, psoriasis or other diagnosed conditions.
